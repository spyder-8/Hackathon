Hikers Discover Remains in Sealed Bunker; Identified as Missing Game Developer

By Marcus Vance | Local Metro Desk

SNOQUALMIE PASS, WA — The King County Sheriff's Office has confirmed that human remains discovered last week by off-trail hikers in the Cascade Mountains belong to Elias Thorne, a software developer who vanished without a trace in 2008.

However, the identification has only deepened the mystery surrounding his decade-long disappearance. According to preliminary reports from the medical examiner's office, the cause of death is utterly inconclusive.

"There are no signs of physical trauma, no evidence of poisoning, no indication of starvation or exposure," stated King County Coroner Dr. Aris Thorne (no relation) in a brief press conference Monday. "Given the state of the location and the remains, we are currently classifying the cause of death as 'Undetermined.' It is a highly unusual case."

The circumstances of the discovery are equally bizarre. Thorne was found inside what authorities describe as an improvised, reinforced bunker buried beneath an abandoned logging road. The heavy steel door was welded shut from the inside.

Inside the windowless 10x10 space, investigators found Thorne seated at a desk, surrounded by four massive, custom-built computer servers. The servers were entirely corroded, seemingly burnt out by a massive electrical surge, despite the bunker having no connection to any external power grid.

Thorne was the lead developer of "Aethelgard's Fall," a highly anticipated indie video game that was abruptly cancelled when he disappeared. Former colleagues reported that Thorne had become dangerously obsessed with the game's code, claiming it was "writing itself" in the weeks leading up to his vanishing.

Police are currently attempting to salvage data from the melted server racks, though technicians have privately expressed doubt that any readable data survived whatever catastrophic event fused the hard drives.
