Acclaimed Indie Sound Designer Reported Missing

Studio left abandoned with equipment melted from the inside out.

Published: November 12, 2018 | By: Sarah Jenkins

Marcus Vance, the highly praised audio engineer known for his experimental synthesized soundscapes, has been officially reported missing by his family after failing to respond to calls for over two weeks.

Vance was last known to be working as a freelance composer for the notoriously troubled RPG developed by Elias Thorne. Friends of Vance claim that in the weeks leading up to his disappearance, he had become obsessed with "compiling the right frequencies" to match Thorne's bizarre environmental lore.

When authorities entered Vance's recording studio, they found it empty. Chillingly, the acoustic foam lining the walls had turned to an unrecognized ash, and his primary hard drives were physically fused together by an intense, localized heat source—though no fire was ever reported.

The last file uploaded to his cloud server was simply titled: "The_Rot_Sings.wav".
