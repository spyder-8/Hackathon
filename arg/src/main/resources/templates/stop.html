Elias Thorne was the sole lead developer, programmer, and narrative designer for the cancelled indie role-playing game Aethelgard's Fall. He founded the independent studio Nebulous Reality in 2007. Thorne is infamous in the indie gaming community for abandoning the highly anticipated project six months into development, and for the subsequent mystery surrounding his disappearance and death.

1. Early Development

Little is known about Thorne's life prior to the announcement of Aethelgard's Fall. He surfaced on various game development forums in late 2007, showcasing a custom-built isometric engine. Former collaborators, all of whom worked remotely on contract, described Thorne as a reclusive workaholic who insisted on coding the game's complex "Celestial Alignment" mechanics entirely on his own. He claimed the game engine was designed to autonomously generate thousands of years of consistent lore based on virtual astronomical movements.

2. 2008 Disappearance

In November 2008, updates on the Nebulous Reality blog ceased. Over the next three weeks, Thorne stopped responding to emails from his contracted artists. By December, the game's servers and website were taken offline due to non-payment. No missing persons report was widely publicized at the time, leading the community to believe Thorne had simply abandoned the project or run out of funds.

3. 2018 Discovery & Death

In September 2018, Thorne's remains were discovered by hikers in the Cascade Mountains, inside a concealed, subterranean concrete bunker. Law enforcement reported that the bunker's heavy steel door had been welded shut from the inside.

Thorne was found seated at a terminal connected to four massively corroded server racks. Despite being sealed in the bunker for approximately ten years, the King County Medical Examiner's office could not determine a cause of death, noting a bizarre lack of standard decomposition. Furthermore, local authorities reported a severe, localized magnetic anomaly within the bunker that destroyed all recoverable data on the servers.

4. Trivia

  • In the v0.4 Alpha build, if the player attempts to look directly at the "Void Moon" without the proper warding spell, the game crashes to the desktop and generates an empty text file named rot.txt.
  • An NPC named 'The Architect', found in the lower catacombs, has a single line of dialogue: "To preserve the world, the creator must seal the vessel from within." Following the 2018 discovery of Thorne's body in a room welded shut from the inside, many fans consider this a disturbing foreshadowing of his fate.
