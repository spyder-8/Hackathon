The Aethelgard Anomaly: The Impossible Death of Elias Thorne

Posted by TruthSeeker_88 | October 03, 2018 | Tags: Unexplained Death Cyber-Mystery

We need to talk about Elias Thorne.

For a decade, the narrative was simple: an indie dev got overwhelmed, faked his death/disappearance, and ran off to a tropical island with the meager kickstarter funds. But the local news just dropped a bombshell, and the official police explanation is insulting our intelligence.

They found him. In a welded-shut bunker. With no cause of death.

I managed to get in touch with a contact who works closely with the King County ME's office. What the news isn't telling you is the state of the body. It wasn't decomposed the way it should have been after 10 years in an un-climate-controlled concrete box in the damp Cascades. My source said it looked like he died yesterday. Mummification? No. It was described as a state of "perfect stasis."

LEAKED CRIME SCENE ANOMALIES:
  • The Door: Welded from the inside. Standard isolation tactic, but no welding equipment was found inside the room. How did he seal it?
  • The Servers: Four massive rigs. Fried. But there was no power source. No generator, no battery array, no solar panels. Just dead iron.
  • The Screens: They found six monitors. The glass on every single one was shattered outward, like they exploded from the inside out.
  • The Code: Thorne's hands were resting on the keyboard. The keys were worn down to the plastic, entirely smooth. Specifically the keys for A, E, T, H, L, G, R, D.

The cops are calling it an "undetermined natural death." I call BS. You don't naturally die in a sealed lead box surrounded by physically impossible computer hardware.

Elias didn't just walk away from Aethelgard's Fall. He tried to lock it in. Or maybe he tried to lock himself in *with* it. The forums back in 2008 were buzzing about his claims that the procedural generation algorithms were creating stuff he didn't write. Lore about celestial bodies aligning.

Look at the stars on the night he vanished. Look at the coordinates of where the bunker was found. I'm crunching the numbers right now. I think Elias didn't die. I think his hardware just couldn't handle the transfer.
